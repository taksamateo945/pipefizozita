    Top Facts about Robert Altman

  • Robert Altman was an American filmmaker born in 1925.
  • He directed over 30 feature films throughout his career.
  • Altman received five Academy Award nominations for Best Director.
  • His most famous film is the ensemble piece “MASH” (1970).
  • Altman’s style often featured overlapping dialogue and improvisation.
  • He was known for his collaborations with actors such as Shelley Duvall and Keith Carradine.
  • Altman won the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences in 2006.
  • His final film, “A Prairie Home Companion,” was released posthumously in 2006.
  • Altman also worked extensively in television, directing episodes of popular shows like “Bonanza” and “Combat!”
  • He passed away at the age of 81 due to complications from leukemia.
